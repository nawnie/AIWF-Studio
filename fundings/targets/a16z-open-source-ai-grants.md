# a16z Open Source AI Grants

Reference page: https://a16z.com/announcing-our-latest-open-source-ai-grants/

## Fit

Good fit if framed as open-source AI infrastructure. Weaker fit if framed as an end-user image-generation app.

Best angle:

- Local AI tooling.
- Hardware/runtime orchestration.
- Fine-tuning and model operation workflows.
- Open-source infrastructure for developers using local models.

## What You Need

- Updated public repo and README.
- Crisp technical narrative.
- Evidence of real adoption or at least serious code maturity.
- Public roadmap issues.
- A short founder/building history.
- A way to contact or be introduced to the grants program, because the public path may be less direct than AI Grant.

## Suggested Ask

Do not lead with a dollar amount unless the intake form asks. Lead with the project and the specific open-source work that funding unlocks.

## Pitch Angle

AIWF Studio is local AI infrastructure for consumer hardware. It gives open-source builders a structured way to run image generation, video generation, model ops, local chat, and training without turning the app into one giant dependency collision.

The interesting part is not only the UI. It is the architecture: typed requests, service routing, backend adapters, process workers, GPU tenant coordination, and optional engines that do not break startup.

## Materials To Prepare

- One-page architecture summary.
- Public demo video.
- Issue roadmap with fundable milestones.
- "Why this is infrastructure" paragraph.
- Comparison table: AIWF Studio vs legacy extension/global-state stacks.

## Milestones To Propose

1. Stable worker/engine interface for image, video, training, and chat.
2. Consumer GPU benchmark suite for local workflows.
3. Documentation and plugin-safe extension points for third-party local engines.

## Caveats

a16z grant posts may not represent an always-open public application. Treat this as a relationship/network target unless a current form is available.
